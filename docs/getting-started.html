Getting Started with Acquisition Pulse

Follow these simple steps to start tracking your true profitability. Setup takes about 5 minutes.

1 Export Your Shopify Order Data

First, you'll need to export your historical order data from Shopify as a CSV file.

  1. Log in to your Shopify admin panel
  2. Navigate to Orders in the left sidebar
  3. Click Export at the top right of the orders page
  4. Select your export options:
    • Export: All orders (or select a date range)
    • Export as: CSV for Excel, Numbers, or other spreadsheet programs
  5. Click Export orders
  6. Shopify will email you a download link within a few minutes
üí° Tip: For best results, export at least 6-12 months of order history. This gives Acquisition Pulse enough data to calculate accurate LTV:CAC ratios and identify trends.

2 Prepare Your CSV File

Acquisition Pulse needs specific columns to calculate profitability. Make sure your CSV includes these fields:

Column Name Description Example
order_date Date the order was placed 2024-01-15
revenue Total order value (excluding tax/shipping) 149.99
cogs Cost of goods sold for this order 67.50
platform Acquisition channel (Google, Meta, TikTok, Organic, etc.) Google
customer_id Unique customer identifier (for cohort analysis) cust_12345
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Shopify exports don't include COGS by default. You'll need to add this manually from your accounting system or calculate it based on your product costs. Use a spreadsheet program to add a cogs column before uploading.

How to add COGS to your export:

  1. Open the exported CSV in Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers
  2. Add a new column called cogs
  3. For each row, enter the total cost of goods for that order
  4. If you have multiple products per order, sum the individual product costs
  5. Save the file as CSV
üí° Tracking acquisition platform: If you use UTM parameters or Shopify's built-in attribution, you can map the Referring site or Landing site columns to platform. Otherwise, you may need to manually tag orders based on your ad campaign tracking.

3 Upload to Acquisition Pulse

Once your CSV is ready, upload it to the dashboard:

  1. Open Acquisition Pulse dashboard (run ./start_dashboard.sh locally or visit your hosted instance)
  2. Navigate to the Data Import section in the sidebar
  3. Click Choose File and select your prepared CSV
  4. Acquisition Pulse will validate the columns and preview your data
  5. Click Import to load the data into the dashboard
üîí Privacy note: Your data is processed locally on your machine. We don't store or transmit your order data to any external servers.

4 Connect Your Google Ads Account

To pull ad spend data automatically, connect your Google Ads account:

  1. In the Acquisition Pulse dashboard, go to Connectors
  2. Click Connect Google Ads
  3. You'll be redirected to Google's OAuth login page
  4. Sign in with the Google account that has access to your Google Ads account
  5. Grant Acquisition Pulse permission to read campaign data (read-only access)
  6. You'll be redirected back to the dashboard
  7. Select your Google Ads account from the dropdown
  8. Click Sync Data to pull campaign spend
üîê Security: Acquisition Pulse uses OAuth 2.0 for authentication. Your Google credentials are never stored. Access tokens are encrypted and stored securely in your system's keyring.
‚ö†Ô∏è Google API Approval: If you're using a newly created Google Ads API developer token, it may be in "test mode" and only work with test accounts. For production accounts, you'll need to apply for Basic or Standard access. This typically requires a privacy policy and website (which you're reading now!).

5 Explore Your Profitability Data

You're all set! Now you can:

  • View profit by channel to see which platforms are actually profitable
  • Analyze LTV:CAC ratios to understand customer lifetime value vs acquisition cost
  • Check margin trends over time to spot seasonal patterns
  • Run cohort analysis to see which months brought the highest-LTV customers
  • Generate profitability reports for stakeholders
üí° Pro tip: Set up a monthly data refresh routine. Export your latest Shopify orders, update COGS, and re-upload to keep your dashboard current.
